Learning with Tensor Networks

by Miles Stoudenmire
in Journal Club on 2016-07-28 | tags: Tensor Networks, Kernel Learning, Quantum

The connection between machine learning and statistical physics has long been appreciated, but deeper relationships continue to be uncovered. For example, techniques used to pre-train neural networks have more recently been interpreted in terms of the renormalization group. In the other direction there has been a sharp increase in applications of machine learning to chemistry, material science, and condensed matter physics which are sources of highly-structured data and could be a good testing ground for machine learning techniques.

A recent trend in both physics and machine learning is an appreciation for the power of tensor methods. In machine learning, tensor decompositions can be used to solve non-convex optimization tasks and make progress on many other important problems while in physics, great strides have been made in manipulating large vectors arising in quantum mechanics by decomposing them as tensor networks.
